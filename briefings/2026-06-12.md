## 1. Jeff Bezos's Prometheus raises $12B to build an 'artificial general engineer' for the physical world

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/11/jeff-bezoss-prometheus-raises-12b-to-build-an-artificial-general-engineer-for-the-physical-world/)  •  12 Jun 2026

Prometheus, co-founded by Jeff Bezos and Vik Bajaj (former co-founder of Verily), raised $12B at a $41B valuation from JPMorgan Chase, Goldman Sachs, BlackRock, and Bezos himself. The startup is building an "artificial general engineer" — software that automates design and manufacturing of complex physical systems, from jet engines to drug compounds. It has 150 employees across San Francisco, London, and Zurich, and plans to devote a large share of the capital to compute.

**Why it matters:** At $41B, this is one of the largest private AI bets ever — targeting the physical-world engineering work that pure software AI hasn't yet touched.

**Thread:** Physical AI is drawing massive capital precisely because investors argue the physical world creates moats that code alone cannot replicate — a different defensibility thesis than software-only AI.

## 2. SpaceX officially prices shares at $135 in the largest IPO ever

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/11/spacex-officially-prices-shares-at-135-in-the-largest-ipo-ever/)  •  11 Jun 2026

SpaceX priced 555.6 million shares at $135 each, raising $75B — the largest IPO in history, surpassing Saudi Aramco's $24.9B 2019 listing. The company will trade as SPCX on Nasdaq. The offering was oversubscribed four times over. Underwriters hold an option for another 83.3 million shares worth ~$11B. A crypto prediction market prices the stock at $167, implying a ~20% opening-day rise. Musk's ~850M Class A shares and 5.6B Class B shares make him the listing's largest beneficiary.

**Why it matters:** The world's biggest IPO ever sets a new ceiling for what public markets will fund for deep-tech, capital-intensive ventures — rewriting what an ambitious technology company can be worth.

**Thread:** SpaceX going public at this scale — the same week Prometheus closes at a $41B private valuation — signals that massive, long-horizon technology infrastructure bets are now attracting capital at nation-state scale.

## 3. Google DeepMind is worried about what happens when millions of agents start to interact

**Source:** [MIT Technology Review](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/06/11/1138794/google-deepmind-is-worried-about-what-happens-when-millions-of-agents-start-to-interact/)  •  11 Jun 2026

Google DeepMind, Schmidt Sciences, the UK's ARIA, the Cooperative AI Foundation, and Google.org are launching a $10M research fund to study multi-agent AI safety. Rohin Shah, who leads DeepMind's AGI safety research, says millions of agents working autonomously together create a new class of risk — supercharged scams, prompt injections, and cyberattacks. Shah estimates only a few more months before agent deployment reaches levels where these risks become real and wants academic research running before that moment arrives.

**Why it matters:** The window to do foundational safety research on multi-agent systems is measured in months, not years — and no such research field yet exists.

**Thread:** Concurrent with this announcement, Anthropic published zero-trust deployment guidelines for agents, suggesting a converging industry view that multi-agent standards need to be built urgently and openly.

## 4. Theker just raised $85M to build the factory robot that doesn't specialize in anything

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/11/theker-just-raised-85m-to-build-the-factory-robot-that-doesnt-specialize-in-anything/)  •  12 Jun 2026

Barcelona-based Theker raised $85M — claimed as Europe's largest-ever robotics Series A — led by CRV, with Samsung and LVMH's investment vehicle among backers. Unlike humanoid robots with a fixed form, Theker's machines can have hands, arms, and overall form swapped for different tasks: sorting packages, packing clothing, or handling warehouse goods. Inditex (Zara's parent) is an early customer and backer. Samsung is in advanced discussions. The company plans to grow from dozens to ~120 employees by year-end.

**Why it matters:** A reconfigurable robot that adapts to different tasks without retraining could unlock automation for the 'messy middle' of factory work that specialized or humanoid robots haven't reached.

**Thread:** Theker's generalist design mirrors the AI field's own shift from narrow specialist models to broad foundation models — betting that adaptability beats deep specialization in physical-world deployment too.

## 5. These are the countries moving to ban social media for children

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/11/social-media-ban-children-countries-list/)  •  11 Jun 2026

More than a dozen countries — including Australia (implemented December 2025), France, Spain, Canada, Denmark, Germany, Greece, Indonesia, Malaysia, Poland, Slovenia, Turkey, and the UK — have announced or are legislating bans on social media for children under 15 or 16. Australia's law covers Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, X, YouTube, and others, with fines up to $49.5M AUD for non-compliance. Austria, Denmark, and Spain are targeting mid-2026 implementation. Canada's bill may take a year to pass.

**Why it matters:** A coordinated global regulatory wave is forcing social media platforms to build age-verification infrastructure at scale — a new design and compliance constraint for every consumer app targeting young users.

**Thread:** What began as Australia's first-mover experiment in late 2025 has become a global product requirement in under six months — any consumer platform must now plan for under-16 verification as a baseline feature.
