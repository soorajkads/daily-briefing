## 1. Statement on US government directive to suspend access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5

**Source:** [Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/fable-mythos-access)  •  13 Jun 2026

The US government invoked national security authorities to issue an export control directive forcing Anthropic to immediately suspend all access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5 — affecting all customers worldwide. The directive claims a jailbreak method was found. Anthropic disputes the severity, stating the vulnerability is narrow, non-universal, and that the same capability level is available from other deployed models including OpenAI's GPT-5.5. Anthropic is complying but explicitly disagrees with the decision.

**Why it matters:** The first time the US government has used export control authority to force a commercial frontier AI model offline for all users — setting a potential precedent for state control of AI deployments.

**Thread:** Directly linked to today's open-source AI piece — the abrupt shutdown underlines why access to open-weight AI infrastructure matters as a hedge against centralized control.

## 3. Open source AI must win

**Source:** [opensourceaimustwin.com](https://opensourceaimustwin.com/?share=v2)  •  13 Jun 2026

The piece argues AI has become "civilizational infrastructure" for work, education, science, and public services — and that access must not depend on closed APIs, shifting terms, opaque moderation, or prices set by a handful of companies. The author calls for AI that remains locally deployable, reproducible, auditable, and community-governed regardless of what dominant labs or cloud platforms do, framing it as a matter of "operational freedom" analogous to the stakes of owning infrastructure itself.

**Why it matters:** Frames closed AI dependency as a structural risk for any organization or nation building on rented cognition — the argument for open infrastructure becomes harder to dismiss after today's Anthropic model shutdown.

**Thread:** Today's US directive against Fable 5 and Mythos 5 is the clearest live demonstration of the fragility this piece warns against.

## 4. Brain-inspired chip runs near absolute zero and could transform quantum computing

**Source:** [Science Daily / University of Hong Kong](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/06/260612032024.htm)  •  12 Jun 2026

Researchers at the University of Hong Kong built a neuromorphic chip that operates at 10mK — near absolute zero — using a silicon carbide transistor repurposed to fire spikes like biological neurons, using thousands of times less power than conventional electronics. Quantum computers require control chips at millikelvin temperatures, but today's silicon chips generate too much heat and must be positioned away from the qubits, creating long wiring. This chip could sit alongside quantum processors, enabling better error correction and real-time quantum control at scale.

**Why it matters:** Removes a key engineering bottleneck in scaling quantum computers — the heat generated by control electronics — using materials already mass-produced for EVs and power grids.

**Thread:** A hardware-layer capability shift that complements the software and governance debates dominating AI this week; quantum control at scale changes what's computable within a decade.

## 5. Mistral is rumored to be raising €3B at €20B valuation

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/12/mistral-is-rumored-to-be-raising-e3b-at-e20-valuation/)  •  12 Jun 2026

French AI lab Mistral is in early discussions to raise about €3 billion ($3.5 billion), per Bloomberg, at a valuation of roughly €20 billion — nearly double its €11.7 billion Series C from September 2025. Mistral has differentiated by offering open-weight models and positioning itself as a "sovereign" European alternative. It is building a data center near Paris and has signed deals with France's army, the government of Luxembourg, and major European companies. To date Mistral has raised only about $4 billion total, far less than US rivals.

**Why it matters:** Would make Mistral Europe's most valuable AI company and signals that sovereign AI — open, non-US, locally deployable — is attracting serious capital precisely when US labs are being restricted.

**Thread:** On the same day the US blocks foreign access to Anthropic's top models, Mistral's pitch as an open, sovereign European alternative becomes significantly more commercially relevant.
