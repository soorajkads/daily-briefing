## 2. The hands-off era of AI oversight is ending. What comes next?

**Source:** [Christian Science Monitor](https://www.csmonitor.com/USA/Politics/2026/0610/artificial-intelligence-regulation-trump-congress)  •  10 Jun 2026

U.S. AI oversight is entering a new phase simultaneously on two tracks. President Trump signed an executive order asking frontier AI companies to voluntarily submit their most powerful models for a 30-day government review before release. On June 4, a bipartisan House draft — the Great American AI Act — proposed mandatory third-party audits for AI developers with over $500M revenue and protections for workers displaced by AI, but also a three-year preemption of state AI development laws. The preemption clause has drawn sharp opposition from Democratic lawmakers and civil liberties groups.

**Why it matters:** The next few months decide whether the US gets one federal AI compliance framework or fifty competing state regimes — a decision that sets the risk surface and cost structure for every AI product built or sold in America.

**Thread:** The week's regulatory moves — this US congressional draft, yesterday's German court ruling making Google liable for false AI summaries, and the EU Act deadline below — together mark the end of AI's largely unregulated era globally.

## 3. Scientists identify hidden T-cell 'brake' SLAMF6 that explains why immunotherapy fails

**Source:** [Medical Daily (Nature)](https://www.medicaldaily.com/slamf6-cancer-immunotherapy-discovery-nature-2026-475575)  •  9 Jun 2026

Researchers at Université de Montréal publishing in Nature on June 9 identified SLAMF6 — a receptor on T cells that self-activates by binding to copies of itself, sending a stop signal that suppresses cancer-killing activity without any signal from the tumour. This mechanism is entirely distinct from PD-1 and CTLA-4 inhibitors, which require the tumour to send a signal first. Custom monoclonal antibodies that block SLAMF6 outperformed existing approaches in mouse models and showed promising synergy with PD-1 drugs, potentially reaching the roughly 40–50% of solid tumours where current immunotherapy fails.

**Why it matters:** A tumour-independent immune checkpoint opens a new category of cancer drug — stackable on top of existing therapies and potentially applicable to the half of solid tumours that don't respond to any current treatment.

**Thread:** The decade-long story of immune checkpoint therapy — PD-1 blockers transformed cancer treatment — now has a next chapter: SLAMF6 antibodies for patients who exhaust existing options, with clinical trials likely 3–5 years away.

## 4. EU AI Act fully applicable August 2026 — compliance deadline looms for global AI builders

**Source:** [European Commission](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai)  •  10 Jun 2026

The EU AI Act reaches full applicability on August 2, 2026 — less than two months away. The transparency obligations that activate on that date require all AI chatbots to disclose they are AI, mandate labeling of AI-generated content including deepfakes, and impose documentation requirements on generative AI providers. High-risk AI in healthcare, employment, education, and critical infrastructure gains a longer runway under the AI Omnibus simplification — December 2027 for most categories. A new prohibition on AI 'nudification' apps generating non-consensual intimate content was also added as part of the Omnibus agreement reached in May.

**Why it matters:** August 2 is a hard compliance switch for any AI product with European users — chatbot disclosure, AI-content labeling, and GPAI transparency documentation move from voluntary to mandatory in weeks.

**Thread:** Counterpoint to the US regulatory debate above: Europe's law-based approach is now fully live, creating a bifurcated global compliance landscape that any cross-border AI product must navigate simultaneously.

Defense-tech startups have raised $14.6 billion in the first five months of 2026, already surpassing the previous full-year record of $9.6 billion set in 2025. The surge concentrates in AI-enabled military systems, autonomous aircraft and naval vessels, and dual-use space infrastructure. Anduril led with a $5 billion Series H, followed by Shield AI's $2 billion Series G and Saronic's $1.75 billion Series D. **Why it matters:** Defense technology has crossed from niche to mainstream VC category — the capital concentration signals a structural bet that autonomous systems and AI-enabled warfare will reshape global military procurement, opening a new market for dual-use technology founders.

**Thread:** As SpaceX prices its record IPO today, Anduril and dozens of other defense-tech startups are watching whether public markets will absorb the next generation of deep-tech defense companies after Swarmer's debut.
